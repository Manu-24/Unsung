UnSung

“Success is not final, failure is not fatal: it is the courage to continue that counts.”
―WINSTON CHURCHILL
Every time you start writing any new chapter. Once again, you must outline what you intend to do with and pitch it.

Now, at the end of every chapter, you left your readers absolutely compelled to turn the page and begin next chapter.
Well, don't disappoint them.
Make them feel very happy to have turned the page.
It is suggested you plan on starting the chapter by giving them something unexpected.
Grab your reader's sense of intrigue from the end of previous chapter and escalate it.
You don't necessarily need to just continue on the same path line from the end of chapter one. There was a reason to end your first chapter where you did, and there must be a compelling reason to begin a new chapter.

Whatever the stakes are, you need to raise them.

This may be sounding like you should be writing an action thriller, but that's not what I'm saying.
Writing in this way applies as much to a novel about internal annexed as it does to a sci-fi space war.
A love story can and should be every bit as intriguing as a spy thriller.

It's all about engagement. Keep the reader engaged.
With that in mind, Write your next chapters and complete your book.

HAPPY WRITING!
