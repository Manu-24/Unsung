UnSung

“Daring ideas are like chessmen moved forward. They may be beaten, but they may start a winning game.”
― Johann Wolfgang von Goethe
To begin, you need to come up with an idea that will become your own intellectual property, or IP, as the basis for your novel.

Some of you may already have an idea for your IP. For others, the question you may be asking yourself is what should I write about?
A very good question.
And there's a simple answer.
You should write about anything at all, anything.

1...You take any idea and develop it into something plausible.
2...Developing the idea is what's important. Moreso than the idea itself, really. 3...Take any idea and just start writing about it.

EXAMPLE

An author named Nicholson Baker wrote an entire novel about a 30 second escalator ride.
You can start with something like I love sandwiches.
So you've just moved to new city and you've been invited to a party in an older high-rise hotel.
Before you leave, you have a craving for a sandwich but the only bread you have is a stale. It's rock hard.
So you think it might be okay if you toast it.
But when you try to slice the baguette, it's so hard that your knife slips off and slices your thumb instead.
Now, you should go to the doc. But you just put a band-aid on it, which continues to leak blood.
You go to the party. You shake a lot of hands and some people give you a welcome hug. And one of them is a nurse.
She turns out to be a drug dealer who gets killed in a drive-by shooting that night. And some of your blood was found on her clothing.
The police track your DNA. You get sent to prison. You become part of a prison gang and...., I don't know, sounds like it has some potential.

And it started out as a story about your passion for sandwiches.

The point is that you never know how good an idea might be until you start to develop it.
So now pitch your idea, that is, write it up in just a paragraph or a few sentences.
And remember, any idea can be developed into something really good.
